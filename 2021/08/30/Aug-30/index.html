8 Things on EADAM (1)

Posted on 2021-08-30 In Summary Views:

This posts servers as a survey on the Efficiency-adjusted deferred acceptance mechanism (EADAM) firstly introduced by Kesten (2010) in his paper School choice with consent. I would cover issues on its original definition, different perspectives of implementation, efficiency, stability, equilibrium and simulation.

1. Kesten(2010)'s orginal definition

Before introduing the algorithm, we would need one key concept brought by Kesten:

1.1 Definition of Interruptor: Suppose there is a student i, who is tentatively accepted by a school s at step t, and later rejected by school s at step t’($ t’ > t$), meanwhile, if there is some student j, who is rejected by s at step l, $t \leq l < t’$, then we call student i an interruptor and say that $(i,s)$ form an interrupting pair of step t’.

1.2 Remark: An interruptor can be interpreted as someone who interrupts the favorable settlement among other students without any gain to himself.

1.3 Remark: If the outcome of SOSM is not efficient, then there must be at least one interrupting pair in the DA process. However, the existence of interrupting pair does not imply efficiency loss.

The essence of the algorithm is to try to recover the welfare loss casued by the interruptors in a right sequence. Due to the constraint efficiency of SOSM1, we know any Pareto improvement would result in priority violation (of the interruptor), hence, it is imperative that we solicit the consent of students before we make any welfare imrprovement.

1.4 The Efficiency-adjusted deferred acceptance mechanism (EADAM):

Round 0: Run the DA algorthim.

Round k ($k \geq 1$): If there are no interrupting pairs or no consenting interruptors, then stops. Otherwise, find the last step of the DA procedure of round $k-1$, where a consenting interruptor is rejected by the school he forms interrupting pair with. Identify all the interrupting pair with consenting interruptor of that step. For each interrupting pair with consent $(i,s)$, drop school s from i’s preference list2 without changing the relative order of the other remaining schools. Rerun DA with the new preference profile.

1. Here, we consider all priorities to be strict. Variants of EADAM can be applied to recover welfare loss due to tie-breaking without seeking consent of a student if he is interruptor due to the specific tie-breaking rule. Hopefully we can talk about this variant and extension in the future. ↩
